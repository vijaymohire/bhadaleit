## 1. Overview

The **QAI Product Foundry** is the central engineering, simulation, verification, and lifecycle execution environment for all Quantum AI products under BhadaleIT.  
It receives validated research output from the Research Division and R&D Lab, transforming concepts into engineered systems, prototypes, simulations, and release-ready QAI components.

The Foundry implements a structured onion-architecture lifecycle spanning theory → design → simulation → engineering → verification → release.

---

## 2. Mission

To convert Quantum AI research into engineered, validated, secure, and production-ready systems through standardized lifecycle processes, multi-domain engineering workflows, and advanced simulation environments.

---

## 3. Objectives

- Provide the engineering backbone for QAI processors, OS, datacenters, sensing, communication, and hybrid AI–quantum systems.  
- Deliver structured simulation environments (Virtual Foundry) to accelerate development.  
- Execute cross-domain engineering streams: hardware, firmware, software, networking, QAI engineering, security, and control.  
- Interface with fabrication partners for specialized hardware builds.  
- Maintain lifecycle consistency with maturity scoring, verification gates, and QAI readiness assessments.  
- Supply release-ready assets to In-House Development and Commercial Divisions.

---

## 4. Role Within the BhadaleIT Ecosystem

    QAI Research Hub  →  R&D Lab  →  QAI Product Foundry  →  Engineering Outputs
    (theory)             (applied R&D)   (engineering)          (MVPs, PoCs, releases)

### The Foundry receives:
- Applied research artifacts  
- Simulation-ready models  
- Lab experimental data  
- Patent concepts & system designs  

### The Foundry produces:
- Engineered prototypes  
- Lifecycle maturity reports  
- Virtual Foundry simulations

## 5. Foundry Structure

The Foundry is divided into five major engineering and lifecycle domains:

1. **Theory & Translation**  
2. **Simulation & Virtual Foundry**  
3. **Core Engineering**  
4. **Fabrication Interface**  
5. **Lifecycle Management**

Each domain aligns with the QAI Computing Lifecycle Framework v1.3.

## 7. Domain Responsibilities

### 7.1 Theory & Translation
- Convert research concepts into technical engineering requirements  
- Perform feasibility evaluation  
- Prepare architectural blueprints and system-level diagrams  
- Collaborate with R&D Lab for conceptual clarification  

---

### 7.2 Simulation & Virtual Foundry
- Maintain simulation engines, digital twins, and virtual testbeds  
- Run lifecycle simulations for QAI processors, OS, models, and datacenters  
- Generate maturity scoring and readiness data  
- Prepare simulation reports for engineering teams  

---

### 7.3 Core Engineering
- Hardware Engineering: QAI processor subsystems, control hardware, analog interfaces  
- Firmware Engineering: FPGA logic, quantum control firmware, synchronization  
- Software Engineering: QAI OS, drivers, microservices, protocol layers  
- QAI Engineering: hybrid models, cognitive functions, autonomous control  
- Network Engineering: QKD, entanglement routing, QAI communication fabrics  
- Security Engineering: PQC, zero-trust, blockchain verification  

Each engineering stream maintains its internal sub-pipelines and documentation.

---

### 7.4 Fabrication Interface
- Prepare fabrication-ready specs (layout, masks, interfaces)  
- Collaborate with partner labs, universities, and commercial fabs  
- Integrate hardware verification feedback into engineering cycles  
- Track environment assumptions: cooling, vacuum, cryo, EMI/EMF shielding  

---

### 7.5 Lifecycle Management
- Manage maturity assessments (QRL, QAI readiness, engineering completeness)  
- Run verification and validation gates  
- Package engineering releases (alpha, beta, stable)  
- Produce audits, release notes, and compliance documentation  
- Coordinate with TTO, IP Division, and In-House Development  

---

## 8. Standard Workflow

    1. Research Transfer (from R&D Lab)
    2. Technical Translation & Architecture
    3. Simulation & Modeling (Virtual Foundry)
    4. Core Engineering Streams
    5. Verification & Validation
    6. Fabrication Readiness (if required)
    7. Release Packaging
    8. Handoff to In-House Development
    9. Commercialization (via Commercial Ops)

This ensures consistent, transparent progression from concept → engineered product.

---

## 9. Integration Rules

### With R&D Lab
- Accept only validated, documented, pre-reviewed research artifacts  
- Provide simulation results and engineering feedback back to research teams  

### With IP Division
- Submit architecture diagrams, engineering specs, and implementation details for IP updates  

### With Fabrication Partners
- Share only sanitized, fabrication-ready technical packages  
- Maintain strict confidentiality and compliance  

### With In-House Development
- Provide stable releases, test specs, and integration documentation  

---

## 10. Governance & Compliance

- Engineering work follows internal QAI standards, security rules, and lifecycle controls  
- Simulation data must be reproducible and traceable  
- All engineering artifacts must be version-controlled  
- Export-controlled components must follow relevant jurisdiction rules  
- Sensitive fabrication data must use encrypted storage
